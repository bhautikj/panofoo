Multiple images

Multiple images can be arranged into a scene, but require a more complex embed code. A vrEmbed story is made up of a top level, single <story> tag which has one or more <scene> tags within it. Each scene can contain as many <photo> tags as you like; each photo contains a code of where to find the image as well as where it goes in the scene. See the result here.
<div>
 <story>
  <scene>
   <photo src="http://vrembed.org/src/assets/byobw_2014_a.jpg" isStereo="false" sphereParams="100,66,60,0"/>
   <photo src="http://vrembed.org/src/assets/byobw_2014_b.jpg" isStereo="false" sphereParams="100,66,-60,-20"/>
  </scene>
 </story><script async src="//vrEmbed.org/vrEmbed.min.js" charset="utf-8"></script>
</div>

Placing images and GUI elements in a scene

Adding captions

Adding captions and text to your scene is easy! In the scene tag, add a text tag; specify it's location using sphereParams and encose your text within the tag. At this stage the text box sizes to fit the horizontal field-of-view specified in sphereParams. You can see more options to style your text boxes here. Take a look at the result here.
<div>
 <story>
  <scene>
   <photo src="http://vrembed.org/src/assets/byobw_2014_a.jpg" isStereo="false" sphereParams="100,66,-20,0"/>
   <text sphereParams="45,30,25,10"/>BRING YOUR OWN BIG WHEEL: every year on Easter Sunday San Francisco locals turn out in droves at the actual, most crooked street in the city for the annual BYOBW races. On a variety of pre-made, home-made and soon-to-be-unmade contraptions they race down in costume. There's no winner but no losers either; you won't find a single face that isn't smiling and having an incredibly good time.</text>
  </scene>
 </story><script async src="//vrEmbed.org/vrEmbed.min.js" charset="utf-8"></script>
</div>

Multiple scenes

Multiple sets of images can be arranged into multiple scenes to build a story. You can name these scenes by adding a name="scene name" qualifier to the <scene> tag. You can then create a visual hyperlink to these scenes by using a <jump> tag in the scene. Put the description for the jump bentwee the <jump> and </jump> tags. See the result here.
<div>
 <story>
  <scene name="Corner Crashes">
   <photo src="http://vrembed.org/src/assets/byobw_2013_a.jpg" isStereo="false" sphereParams="100,66,60,-10"/>
   <photo src="http://vrembed.org/src/assets/byobw_2013_b.jpg" isStereo="false" sphereParams="100,66,-60,0"/>
   <jump sphereParams="35,30,30,-30" jumpTo="Finish line">Finish line</jump>
  </scene>
  <scene name="Finish line">
   <photo src="http://vrembed.org/src/assets/byobw_2014_a.jpg" isStereo="false" sphereParams="100,66,60,0"/>
   <photo src="http://vrembed.org/src/assets/byobw_2014_b.jpg" isStereo="false" sphereParams="100,66,-60,-20"/>
   <jump sphereParams="40,30,-20,-20" jumpTo="Corner crashes">Corner crashes</jump>
  </scene>
 </story><script async src="//vrEmbed.org/vrEmbed.min.js" charset="utf-8"></script>
</div>
